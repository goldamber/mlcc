Meet Angel

Today we welcome our new resident Angel
She was raised in captivity. However, her owners didn't treat her properly since they didn't know what kind of environment she needed. Common bottlenose dolphins can travel hundreds of kilometers a day. Angel's pool was too small for her.
Now she stays with us and seems to recover. We do our best to make her feel better. We are planning to start a release program for Angel which includes proper training.

Luna is now free

Today we celebrate the release of our favourite sea lion Luna
She became one of our shelter animals when she was a young adult and had to stay with us for 5 month in order to recover. Thanks to your donations her recovery didn't take long. She became great friends with our stuff members. Everybody will niss her now.
Glad to know that she has successfully joined the pack of wild sea lions which seemed to be her family. Enjoy your freedom Luna!

Found a newborn orca of southern resident pod

We welcome a new member of southern resident killer whale pod
Research program is one of MLCC activities. Today our researchers discovered a newborn orca calf. This is unique because southern resident killer whales are the most endangered subspecies of orca. Because of this every single member is extremely important for survival of this kind.
This is a great achievement for both scientists and killer whales!
